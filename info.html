History of Kokoda

The Kokoda track is a 96km single file track through the Owen Stanley Range. This stood as the primary location of a series of 1942 battles between the Japanese and primarily Australian Allied forces in the then Australian territory of Papua. This battle would later be named the Kokoda Track Campaign which saw the end of the Japanese' southern advance. Approximately 43000 soldiers fought in the campaign and over 12000 lives were lost across both sides, with some fallen soldiers buried along the track. We walk this trek to honour the fallen, their sacrifice and their memory.


Health and Visa Requirements

The Kokoda Track is 96km long at a peak altitude of 2190m. To walk this trek we recommend that you be fit and healthy. Many bugs live in the tropics of Kokoda and can carry disease, so we recommend you recieve the necessary vaccinations from your healthcare professional, for an idea of what is required go here. We recommend bringing strong bug repellant and plenty of sunscreen. We also recommend checking your government's current travel warning before your adventure to ensure your safety. The Australian Government Smart Traveller website can be found here.

A visa is required to enter Papua New Guinea, to find out more info and apply for your visa go here.
